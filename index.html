[Weather Diary]
Clear skies this morning. The city finally feels like it’s officially in spring—flowers in bloom, trees budding, and pigeons marching about. Dom and I took a walk to the promenade and had lunch facing the water. We had just looked through Rinko Kawauchi’s Illuminance and the soft light bouncing off the surface of the water brought to my mind her photo of three pigeons suspended against a backdrop of illuminated water.

Since birds are built to detect the slightest changes in air pressure, they make quite reliable storm forecasters. In New York, storms will often push rare migratory birds to fly inland so birders have learned to anticipate storms.

Blue skies early in the morning that quickly disappeared under thick clouds. Another day at the Red Cross, trying to rack up some hours before I leave for Austin on Monday. Mauricio stopped by to check on Jim and me and mentioned that the number of fires should be going down with the warmer weather. He said that winter storms always bring the worst fires.

Fast moving clouds in the sky this morning. I went out with Jerry on my Red Cross shift, and he told me about the time they responded to the 2009 Hudson River plane crash. The passengers were split up between the ferry dock and a casino cruise ship where they were given food and blankets. After Hurricane Sandy, he went out the next day to assess flood damage in the Rockaways. They measured exterior water lines on the houses and sent the damage assessment to Red Cross HQ.
